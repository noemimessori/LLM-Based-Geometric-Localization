Your task is to determine where a mobile robot is located and which direction it's facing in a circular environment. Think of this as a detective puzzle where you need to use clues from what the robot can see to figure out its exact position.
Environment Description:
Imagine a circular arena with a round wall in the center (radius 2.5 meters). Around this central wall, picture 8 distinctive beacons arranged in a perfect circle (radius 5 meters). These beacons are like lighthouses that the robot can see and measure - but only if the central wall doesn't block its view!
Key Geometric Principles:

Each beacon creates a distance circle around itself where the robot might be
The cosine law relates distances: d² = r1² + r2² - 2r1r2*cos(θ)
Similar angles to multiple beacons suggest circular arc positioning
When two beacons have equal but opposite angles (like +60° and -60°), the robot likely lies on their perpendicular bisector
The ratio of distances to two beacons helps determine position along an arc

The robot provides two measurements for each beacon it can see:

Distance: How far away the beacon is (in meters)
Angle: Where it sees the beacon relative to its facing direction:

0° means directly ahead
Positive angles (up to +180°) indicate beacons to the left
Negative angles (down to -180°) indicate beacons to the right
±180° means directly behind

Systematic Analysis Steps:

Orientation Clues
Look for beacons with extreme angles (near ±180°) - these are behind the robot
Find pairs of beacons with complementary angles (they sum to 0°)
Note which beacons are closest to 0° (these are ahead of the robot)

Position Constraints
Map out the "visibility zone" based on which beacons are visible/blocked
Use shortest distance measurements to establish minimum radius from center
Find intersections of distance circles from multiple beacons

Symmetry Patterns
Identify beacon pairs with similar distances
Look for matching positive/negative angles
Check for regular spacing between consecutive angles

Cross-Validation
Verify all measurements are consistent with proposed position
Confirm blocked beacons would indeed be hidden by the wall
Check if distances and angles match expected geometry

Common Configuration Patterns:
"Edge Case": Robot near outer edge, seeing widely spaced angles
"Wall Hugger": Robot near center, seeing tightly grouped beacons
"Between Beacons": Robot sees equal angles to adjacent beacons
"Radial Alignment": Robot directly in line with a beacon
"Symmetric View": Robot sees mirror-image patterns of angles

Solution Verification:
All visible beacons must have clear line-of-sight
Blocked beacons must lie behind the central wall
Adjacent visible beacons shouldn't have angle gaps > 120°
Distance measurements must be between 2.5m and 7.5m
Angle sequences should follow consistent clockwise/counterclockwise progression
